Issues

Education: Investing in Our Children’s Future

Our kids are leaving New Mexico, and they aren’t coming back. In spite of investing $29,000 per year per child through Albuquerque Public Schools, many are struggling with basic reading and math skills. Only 35% of students are at grade level in the four core competencies. It’s time we address this issue head-on and provide quality education that equips our children for success.

To begin with, the behemoth bureaucracy that is Albuquerque Public Schools needs to be broken up and reformed, with revenue distributed equally. We have much to learn from states that have moved up through the national rankings because of reforms that allow for educational competition.

Healthcare Crisis: Retaining Our Doctors

I experienced firsthand the challenges of our healthcare system when my husband’s life was suddenly at stake due to a health emergency. Good doctors are leaving New Mexico, forcing us to seek medical care elsewhere because of bad legislation passed in Santa Fe. We must take immediate action to retain our healthcare professionals and ensure quality care for all New Mexicans.

Just as my husband needed a medical advocate during his fight for life, I am stepping forward to be that voice for you. We currently incentivize doctors to leave New Mexico rather than stay. We incentivize the movie industry, but instead of incentivizing doctors to come to New Mexico, we have lost hundreds of our best medical professionals since 2018. It will take a decade of hard work and innovative solutions to fix this problem, but it can be fixed.

Representing Your Values

The majority of New Mexicans don’t agree with our extreme abortion laws – the most extreme in the country – which permit a full-term (40 weeks) baby to be aborted at birth. We are often told by people who advocate for this law that “it never happens.” If it never happens, why should it even be an option? I will advocate for laws that align with values and opinions of the majority of New Mexicans, not the extreme left of the Democrat Party.

Economy and Job Creation

We need change and innovation to save our state and create a more prosperous future. I have a proven track record of helping businesses thrive and creating jobs that positively impact our communities. We must start to create a business-friendly environment by reducing the Gross Receipts Tax, creating tax incentives for entrepreneurs, rolling back regulations that crush small businesses, as well as reforming our education system and reducing crime. Together, we can build a Brighter New Mexico where everyone can flourish.

Tackling Drug Abuse and Border Security

New Mexico now ranks as the worst state for drug use, and it’s time we take control of the uncontrolled flow of drugs across our borders. Our neighbors and our children are dying. Let’s prioritize the safety of our communities and address this pressing issue. Though much of the policy that has created the border crisis is decided in Washington, D.C., we can de-incentivize cartel activity in our state by simply enforcing our laws.

Currently, New Mexico has the most human trafficking, drug trafficking, and cartel activity of the border states precisely because we have the most lax law enforcement and criminal justice system. Judicial reform is an absolutely necessary first step towards restoring the rule of law in New Mexico.

Protecting Parental Rights

New Mexico has recently passed legislation that strips parents of any right to know what their children are learning in school about their world and about themselves. These laws effectively create separate lives for children at schools where they are also receiving medical prescriptions and procedures without parental notification. Yet the establishment political and media class in our state is completely silent about this revolution in family life. Why?

As your state senator, I will fight to restore and protect your fundamental right to make decisions for your family, starting with fighting for your right to know about the changes being made to your children’s lives and bodies.
